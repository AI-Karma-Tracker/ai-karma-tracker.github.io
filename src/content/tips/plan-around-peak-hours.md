Most AI limits aren't just about *how much* you use - they're about *when*.
During peak demand, shared capacity tightens, models get slower, and per-feature
caps (image generation, deep research, uploads) drain faster.

## When it's tight

Peak tends to land in **US daytime hours** (roughly 1-7pm ET across providers).
If you're in Europe or Asia, your evening is their crunch.

## Three quick wins

1. **Batch heavy jobs off-peak.** Long research runs and image batches are much
   happier in your early morning.
2. **Take the nudge.** When a peak-hour alert fires, that's a good moment to
   step away - you'll come back to a fresh window.
3. **Split models.** If one model's per-feature cap is low, move routine work to
   a lighter model and save the expensive one for when it matters.

The extension flags peak hours per provider and nudges you to rest, so you spend
your best limits on your best work.
